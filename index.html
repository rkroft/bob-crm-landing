So… what is Bob?

Bob is a network management system (NMS) I built for myself — a memory for the network I care about. Investors, founders, friends, clients, professors, former colleagues, friends-of-friends, "the person someone swore I had to meet."

Similar tools (CRM) are built to sell to people. Bob is built to keep up with them, and to help me authentically connect over time. It tracks the things that actually matter in a relationship: who connected us, what came of it, when we last spoke, what help they need or what's on their mind, and who I should reach out to next.

How it works

Capture → Enrich → Connect

Bob runs in the background. I just talk to it, and it does the filing.

01
💬

Capture

I text Bob over WhatsApp like a friend: "met Sam at the dinner, Lia intro'd us." It also reads my calendar and inbox to catch the intros I forgot to log.

02
✨

Enrich

Every morning Bob fills in the blanks (title, company, LinkedIn, city) and researches the gaps. It flags anything it's unsure about instead of guessing.

03
🕸️

Connect

Bob maps the whole network: who knows whom, which communities cluster together, who my super-connectors are. Then it nudges me toward the next good hello, and the introductions I should be making.

The WhatsApp brain

Bob lives in my texts

No app to open, no forms. I text Bob like a friend — it logs new people, sends a weekly digest of what it captured and what needs my eyes, and nudges me toward the people I've gone cold on.

Why I built it

My network is the work

I teach, I consult, and I invest: three careers running at once, and every one of them runs on people. Founders, students, operators, fellow investors, the friends who connect me to all of them. Over the years that's added up to a lot of relationships across a lot of different rooms. The relationships are the job. But the tools for keeping up with them were either glorified sales pipelines or a tangle of half-remembered email threads. Nothing knew the story: who introduced whom, what an intro turned into, who I owed a thank-you.

So I built the thing I actually wanted. Bob doesn't try to sell anyone anything. It just helps me be a better friend, connector, colleague, and investor, to show up for the people who show up for me, and to never let a good introduction go unanswered.

And the best part: I built it conversationally, with Claude Code, piece by piece. No engineering team, no SaaS subscription. Just me describing what I wished existed, and watching it come to life.

Of everything AI could help with, this is what I wanted it pointed at: human relationships. It's the connective tissue between people, who knows whom, who vouched for whom, what came of it, that makes relationships interesting. And relationships don't hold still. People move, roles shift, the thread between two people deepens or goes quiet. You can't evolve that narrative unless you keep up with it.

I wanted a tool that protects that, not one that replaces it.
